Email Phishing

Email phishing is a type of cyber attack where attackers send fraudulent emails that appear to be from legitimate sources to trick recipients into revealing sensitive information.

How to Protect Yourself:

• Check sender's email address carefully

• Look for spelling and grammar errors

• Never click suspicious links

• Verify urgent requests through other channels

Spear Phishing

Spear phishing is a targeted form of phishing where attackers customize their messages for specific individuals or organizations, making them more convincing.

How to Protect Yourself:

• Be cautious of personalized emails

• Verify unusual requests from colleagues

• Use multi-factor authentication

• Keep personal information private

Clone Phishing

Clone phishing involves creating a nearly identical copy of a legitimate email, but with malicious links or attachments, often sent from a spoofed address.

How to Protect Yourself:

• Check email headers carefully

• Verify unexpected attachments

• Use email security tools

• Report suspicious emails

Whaling

Whaling is a type of phishing attack that specifically targets high-profile individuals like executives or senior management to steal sensitive information or money.

How to Protect Yourself:

• Implement strict approval processes

• Train executives on security

• Use secure communication channels

• Verify financial requests
